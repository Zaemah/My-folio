About Me

Name: Emmah Zanele Mpofu

Profession: Software Developer (Front-end) & Social Scientist

Emmah Zanele Mpofu is a talented young professional with a passion for technology, social justice, and community development. She is a certified Software Developer(Frontend) and holds a Master of Science Degree in Development Studies as well as Bachelor of Arts Honors Degrree in Language and Communication Studies from Lupane State University.

Professionally, Emmah works as a freelance Junior Software Developer and has previously worked as a programs officer for a youth organisation, as well as a court interpreter . She is passionate about using her skills and education to make a positive impact in her community.

Emmah is also active in several ambassador programs focused on the UN's Sustainable Development Goals and youth empowerment. She is an ambassador for the One Million Youth Actions Challenge, an SDG Ambassador for Yett-Africa. A member of Activista Zimbabwe and the Humanities Research Institute of Africa organizations aimed at advancing humanities research, activism, and innovation in Africa.

Beyond her professional work, Emmah enjoys academics and activism. She is a gifted scriptwriter, thespian and researcher. Emmah lives by the mantra: "Where my intelligence fails me my determination will carry me through. She is determined to keep learning, growing and finding ways to use her talents to improve the world.

SheCodes: Bridging the Gender Gap in Tech.

However, organizations like SheCodes are working tirelessly to bridge this gap and create a more inclusive environment for women in technology. SheCodes is an online coding workshop that aims to teach women coding skills through intensive workshops. By providing a supportive learning environment and accessible resources, SheCodes empowers women to pursue careers in tech. Through their workshops, they equip women with the necessary skills to thrive in the industry.

"Dumbed Bottles", a potential fire hazard in natural areas such as velds.

Dumbed bottles are increasingly becoming a potential fire hazard in natural areas such as velds, due to careless disposal practises leading to the accumulation of these combustile materials in natural settings. In addition to creating an aesthetic blight, dumped glass bottles can damage fragile ecosystems, increase the risk of uncontrolled wildfires, and pose a threat to public safety. Unless greater attention is paid to effective disposal of these valuable resources, the risk of poorly managed combustible materials will only incease.

"Did you know that DUMPED WASTE not only poses significant environmental risks but also impedes the achievement of several Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Specifically, SDGs 6, 12, 13, and 15 are directly affected by the improper disposal of waste.

Firstly, SDG 6 aims to ensure access to clean water and sanitation for all. Dumped waste can contaminate water sources, making them unsafe for consumption and use. This pollution hinders progress towards providing clean water to communities and undermines efforts to improve sanitation facilities. Secondly, SDG 12 focuses on responsible consumption and production patterns. Dumped waste signifies a failure in managing resources efficiently. It highlights the need for better waste management systems that promote recycling, reduce landfill usage, and encourage sustainable practices.
